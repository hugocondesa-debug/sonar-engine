# F1 · Valuations across asset classes

> Sub-índice do Ciclo Financeiro — capítulo 7 do manual (Manual_Ciclo_Financeiro_COMPLETO).

### 7.1 Porquê valuations são Layer 1
Valuations são foundation layer do ciclo financeiro. Em cada momento, pode-se perguntar: "quanto estão estes assets a custar, relative to fundamentals ou história?" A resposta define a distância from fair value, e por extensão o potential downside risk.

Keynes reconheceu: "in the long run, markets are weighing machines." Shiller documentou empiricamente: excess volatility in valuations vs fundamentals. Valuations eventualmente reverting to norms é uma das few relações com strong historical support.

> *Critical caveat: valuations are notoriously poor market timers. Expensive markets can get more expensive, sometimes for years. But valuations are excellent indicators of medium-term return distributions.*

### 7.2 Equity valuations — the classics
**CAPE (Shiller PE)**

Cyclically Adjusted Price-Earnings ratio. Shiller's signature metric.

*CAPE = Price / (10-year trailing inflation-adjusted earnings average)*

- Historical range: 5-45 (US)

- Long-run mean: ~17

- Above 25: elevated

- Above 30: bubble territory

- Above 40: extreme (only 2000, 2021, 2025)

- Peak 2000: 44

- Current 2026: monitoring ~35

**Why CAPE vs simple PE**

- Simple PE uses current year earnings — too volatile

- Earnings cyclical; can be temporarily depressed or elevated

- 10-year average smooths cycles

- Better correlation with subsequent returns

- Robert Shiller's Nobel Prize contribution

**Buffett Indicator**

Total market capitalization divided by GDP. Warren Buffett called this "probably the best single measure of where valuations stand at any given moment."

*Buffett = Total Market Cap / Nominal GDP*

- Historical range: 40-200%

- Long-run mean: ~80-100%

- Above 150%: significantly overvalued

- Above 170%: bubble territory

- Peak 2021: ~200%

- Current 2026: monitoring

**Equity Risk Premium (ERP)**

ERP = Expected Return on Equity - Risk-free Rate. Core in modern valuation.

- Calculated various ways (earnings yield - 10Y, DCF-implied, etc.)

- Typical range: 300-600bps

- Below 300bps: expensive

- Below 100bps: extreme (1999 near-zero)

- Above 700bps: cheap historically

- Damodaran implied ERP tracked monthly

**Price-to-Book (P/B)**

- Price per share / Book value per share

- Less reliable since intangible assets dominant

- Still useful for financial sectors

- Historical range: 1-4

**Dividend Yield**

- S&P 500 dividend yield / price

- Historical range: 1-6%

- Below 2%: late cycle typically

- Above 4%: typically after major declines

### 7.3 Bond valuations
**Yield levels**

- 10-year Treasury yield

- Compare to historical ranges

- Compare to inflation expectations

- Real yields (TIPS-implied)

**Term premium**

- Compensation for holding longer-duration

- Historical average ~100bps

- 2010s: sometimes negative

- 2023-25: positive territory return

- Useful indicator for bond attractiveness

**Credit spreads as valuation**

- HY OAS: implied default-adjusted yield

- IG OAS: investment grade premium

- Relative to historical

- Currently tight ends = expensive credit

**Yield curve shape**

- Normal: positive-sloped

- Inverted: recession pricing

- Flat: transition typical

- Steep: early cycle typical

### 7.4 Real estate valuations
**Price-to-income ratio**

- Median home price / median annual income

- Long-run US average: 3-4x

- Above 5x: stretched

- Above 7x: bubble territory (e.g., 2006 California)

- Varies by geography enormously

**Price-to-rent ratio**

- Home price / annual rent

- Historical US average: 15-20

- Above 25: elevated

- Above 30: bubble territory

- Current 2026: elevated many markets

**Cap rates — commercial RE**

*Cap Rate = Net Operating Income / Property Value*

- Historical range: 4-10%

- Low cap rates = expensive

- Below 4%: historical extremes

- Geographic and sector variations massive

**REIT premium/discount**

- REIT price / NAV (net asset value)

- Premium \>0% = trading above NAV

- Discount \<0% = trading below NAV

- Cycles between premiums and discounts

- 2024-25: many sectors at discount

**Mortgage spreads**

- Mortgage rate - 10Y Treasury

- Historical typical: 150-200bps

- Widen during stress

- 2022-23: widened significantly

- Affect affordability

### 7.5 Crypto valuations — native metrics
**MVRV Ratio**

*MVRV = Market Value / Realized Value*

Market value = current market cap. Realized value = sum of (each coin × price when last moved).

- Bubble signal: \> 3.5 sustained

- Bottom signal: \< 1.0 (coins being spent at losses)

- Historical peaks: 4.0+ in 2013, 2017, 2021

- Historical troughs: \< 1.0 at cycle bottoms

- Currently monitoring

**NUPL — Net Unrealized Profit/Loss**

- Fraction of BTC supply in profit

- Expressed as ratio (-1 to 1)

- Above 0.75: high euphoria historical

- Below 0: capitulation zone

**SOPR — Spent Output Profit Ratio**

- Average profit/loss ratio of coins moved each day

- \> 1.0: holders in profit net

- \< 1.0: holders in loss (capitulation signal)

- Useful short-term sentiment

**NVT — Network Value to Transactions**

*NVT = Market Cap / On-chain Transaction Volume*

- Parallel to PE for crypto

- High NVT: overvaluation

- Low NVT: undervaluation

- Used for BTC specifically

**Puell Multiple**

- Daily USD mining issuance / 365-day average

- \> 4.0: mining profitability extreme, bubble

- \< 0.3: miner capitulation

- Historical cycle markers

**Bitcoin dominance**

- BTC market cap / Total crypto market cap

- Falling dominance: alt-coin speculation rising

- Late-cycle indicator

### 7.6 Commodity valuations
**Gold vs inflation**

- Gold price / CPI

- Multi-decade cycles

- Elevated during inflation episodes

**Oil vs demand indicators**

- Relate to industrial activity proxies

- Supply/demand fundamentals

- Futures curve shape (contango/backwardation)

**Copper vs industrial activity**

- "Dr. Copper" as economic indicator

- Price vs global IP

- China demand critical

**Commodity index aggregates**

- Bloomberg Commodity Index

- GSCI

- CRB Index

- Relate to historical averages

### 7.7 Currency valuations
**Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)**

- Big Mac Index — simple intuitive

- OECD PPP — more comprehensive

- Long-run guidance

- Less useful short-term

**REER — Real Effective Exchange Rate**

- Trade-weighted real exchange rate

- Deviation from long-run average

- BIS publishes for major currencies

**Interest rate differentials**

- Yield advantage driving flows

- Short-term determinant

- Can diverge from PPP long periods

### 7.8 F1 Valuations — the composite
**Weighting structure**

| **Asset class**        | **Weight** | **Primary metric**           |
|------------------------|------------|------------------------------|
| Equity valuations      | 35%        | CAPE, Buffett, ERP composite |
| Bond valuations        | 20%        | Term premium, credit spreads |
| Real estate valuations | 20%        | Price/income, cap rates      |
| Crypto valuations      | 10%        | MVRV, NUPL composite         |
| Commodity valuations   | 10%        | Gold/CPI, oil                |
| FX positioning         | 5%         | REER deviations              |

**Z-scoring approach**

- Each metric z-scored on 10-year rolling window

- Percentile rank complementary

- Mean of asset class metrics = asset-class z-score

- Weighted aggregate = F1

**Interpretation**

- F1 \> 75: valuations broadly stretched (Euphoria territory)

- F1 55-75: valuations elevated

- F1 45-55: near normal

- F1 30-45: valuations depressed

- F1 \< 30: valuations extreme bargain (Stress territory)

### 7.9 Limitations of valuations
**Valuations are poor timers**

- Expensive markets can get more expensive

- 1996-2000: CAPE went from 28 to 44

- 2018-2021: elevated whole period

- Can't time tops via valuations alone

**Need context**

- Interest rate environment

- Earnings growth

- Quality of earnings

- Regulatory changes

- Must combine with other layers

**Model risk**

- 10-year CAPE average: what if regime changed?

- Structural factors (rates low, tech scaling)

- Rolling window partially adapts

- But not perfectly
