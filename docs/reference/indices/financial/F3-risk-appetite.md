# F3 · Risk appetite e volatility

> Sub-índice do Ciclo Financeiro — capítulo 9 do manual (Manual_Ciclo_Financeiro_COMPLETO).

### 9.1 Risk appetite — the core driver
Risk appetite é o willingness of investors to hold risky assets. Drives cycles inherent. When risk appetite is high, assets are bid. When risk appetite collapses, assets are sold regardless of fundamentals.

Operational measurement é challenging because risk appetite não é directly observable. But proxies abound: volatility levels, credit spreads, safe haven flows, financial conditions indices.

> *"When the tide goes out you see who was swimming naked." Buffett's observation is exactly about risk appetite cycles. Bull markets hide risks; bear markets expose them.*

### 9.2 VIX — the fear gauge
**What VIX is**

- CBOE Volatility Index

- Implied volatility of S&P 500 options

- 30-day forward-looking

- Expressed in annualized percentage

**Historical range**

- Low regime: 10-15

- Normal: 15-20

- Elevated: 20-30

- Crisis: 30-50+

- All-time high: 82 (October 2008)

- Covid peak: 82 (March 2020)

**Interpretation**

- Low VIX sustained: complacency, late cycle

- Rising VIX: transition signal

- VIX \> 30: Stress mode

- VIX spike then decay: often marks bottom

**VIX structure**

- VIX futures term structure

- Contango (normal): VIX below futures

- Backwardation (stress): VIX above futures

- Backwardation indicates elevated stress

**Related measures**

- VVIX — volatility of VIX

- SKEW — tail risk pricing

- VIX/VXV ratio — term structure signal

### 9.3 Bond volatility — MOVE index
**MOVE index**

- Merrill Option Volatility Estimate

- Treasury option implied volatility

- Bond-specific fear gauge

- Decoupled from VIX sometimes

**Interpretation**

- MOVE low: bond market calm

- MOVE rising: bond market stress

- Typical range: 60-120

- 2022 peak: \>170 (inflation shock)

- Historically rising rates + rising MOVE = stress building

**VIX vs MOVE divergences**

- VIX low, MOVE high: bond-specific stress (rate uncertainty)

- VIX high, MOVE low: equity-specific (earnings concern)

- Both high: genuine crisis

### 9.4 Credit spreads — risk premium
**HY OAS as risk gauge**

- ICE BofA HY OAS

- Historical mean: ~500bps

- \< 300bps: extremely compressed (bubble risk)

- 350-500bps: normal

- 500-700bps: elevated

- \> 700bps: stress

- \> 1000bps: severe distress

- Peak 2008: 2000+ bps

- Peak 2020: 1100bps

**IG OAS**

- More stable than HY

- Historical: 100-200bps

- Above 250bps: significant stress

- Earlier warning than HY sometimes

**BBB Effective Yield**

- Lowest IG

- Near-IG/HY boundary

- Market sensitivity high

- "Fallen angel" risk proxy

**Spread momentum**

- Direction matters more than level sometimes

- Widening sustained: warning

- Narrowing from wide: bullish

### 9.5 Safe haven demand
**USD**

- Global reserve currency

- Bid during stress typically

- Exception: dollar-specific crises (rare)

**JPY**

- Traditional safe haven

- Carry-trade unwinding support

- Role diminished in recent crises

**CHF (Swiss franc)**

- Safe haven currency

- SNB intervention sometimes

- Limited market size

**Gold**

- Classical safe haven

- Inflation hedge

- No yield in low rate environment

- Surged 2022-25 on debasement concerns

**US Treasuries**

- Benchmark safe haven

- 10Y yield compresses in risk-off

- Exception: 2022 (inflation shock)

### 9.6 Financial Conditions Indices
**Chicago Fed NFCI**

- National Financial Conditions Index

- Composite of 105 measures

- Credit, leverage, risk

- Monthly publication

- Positive: tighter than average

- Negative: looser than average

- Widely used by analysts

**Goldman Sachs FCI**

- Equity prices, bond yields, FX, credit

- Weighted combination

- Commercial but conceptually similar

**Bloomberg FCI**

- Similar approach

- Different weights

- Cross-validation tool

**ECB Composite Indicator of Systemic Stress (CISS)**

- European equivalent

- Multi-market stress composite

### 9.7 Crypto-specific risk metrics
**Crypto volatility indices**

- Bitcoin volatility index (BVOL)

- Ethereum volatility (EVOL)

- Deribit-based options metrics

- Extreme versus traditional markets

**Funding rates**

- Perpetual futures funding

- Positive: longs paying shorts (bulls dominant)

- Extremely positive: crowded long, reversal risk

- Negative: bearish positioning

- Daily data, real-time signal

**Put/call ratio for crypto**

- Deribit options data

- Imbalance = sentiment extreme

**Liquidations**

- Leverage blowouts on volatility

- Daily aggregate liquidations

- Peak liquidations mark cycle tops/bottoms

### 9.8 Safe haven demand in crypto era
**Traditional safe havens still dominant**

- USD, gold, JPY, Treasuries

- Institutional mind-set

- Liquidity advantages

**Emerging crypto safe haven debate**

- BTC narrative as digital gold

- Actually: high beta with risk assets typically

- Selective safe haven in specific scenarios

- Not yet institutional safe haven

**Stablecoins as USD proxy**

- USDT, USDC on-chain USD

- Crypto-native safe asset

- Reserves composition matters

### 9.9 F3 Risk Appetite composite
**Structure**

Volatility (30%) + credit spreads (30%) + safe haven demand (15%) + financial conditions (15%) + crypto risk (10%).

**Interpretation**

- F3 \> 75: extreme risk appetite (Euphoria), complacency

- F3 55-75: healthy risk appetite

- F3 30-55: cautious, concern rising

- F3 \< 30: risk-off, stress mode

**Cross-validation checks**

- All three primary (vol, spreads, safe havens) should align

- Divergences flag for review

- 2022: vol elevated, spreads widening, safe havens mixed (bonds fell too)

- Anomaly flagged for policy regime shift
