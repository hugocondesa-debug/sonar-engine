# F4 · Positioning e flows

> Sub-índice do Ciclo Financeiro — capítulo 10 do manual (Manual_Ciclo_Financeiro_COMPLETO).

### 10.1 Por que positioning matter
Positioning é distribuição de exposure aos vários asset classes entre investors. Extreme positioning — quando todos estão do mesmo lado — é frequentemente precedente de reversal.

Paralelo conceptual: sentiment surveys measure opinions, positioning measures actions. Positioning is put your money where your mouth is. Often positioning leads sentiment shifts or confirms them.

> *Classic contrarian indicator: when positioning is extreme one-sided, remaining fuel for further moves exhausted. The market needs new participants, but they've all arrived. Reversal often follows.*

### 10.2 Retail sentiment surveys
**AAII (American Association of Individual Investors)**

- Weekly survey

- Bullish/Neutral/Bearish

- Bull-Bear spread typical range: -30 to +30

- Above +30: extreme bullish (contrarian warning)

- Below -30: extreme bearish (contrarian buy signal)

- Strongly contrarian indicator historically

**Investors Intelligence**

- Weekly survey of newsletter writers

- Bull/Bear/Correction categories

- Percent Bulls minus Percent Bears

- Historical extremes around tops/bottoms

**NAAIM (professional investor exposure)**

- Active managers exposure survey

- Equity allocation percent reported

- More contemporaneous measure

**Consumer confidence specifically asking about stocks**

- Conference Board asks stock price expectations

- Bullish expectations peaks near tops

- Similar to AAII but broader sample

### 10.3 Options positioning
**Put/Call ratios**

- CBOE total P/C: all equity options

- CBOE equity P/C: individual stocks

- CBOE index P/C: index options

- Rising P/C: growing bearishness

- Falling P/C: growing bullishness

- Extremes = contrarian signal

**P/C ratio interpretations**

- Equity P/C: 0.5-0.9 typical range

- Index P/C: higher, used for hedging

- Total P/C: mix of both

- \< 0.5: extreme call buying (bullish extreme)

- \> 1.2: extreme put buying (bearish extreme)

**VIX/VXV ratio**

- VIX (30-day) / VXV (90-day)

- Below 0.9: complacency

- Above 1.0: hedging demand rising

**Skew**

- CBOE SKEW Index

- Measures tail risk pricing

- Higher SKEW: more tail hedging

- Extreme SKEW spikes before corrections

**Gamma exposure**

- Market maker options positioning

- Negative gamma: MMs sell into down moves, amplify

- Positive gamma: MMs buy into down moves, stabilize

- Gamma flips often mark market inflections

### 10.4 COT — Commitments of Traders
**CFTC Commitments of Traders**

- Weekly CFTC report

- Commercial vs Non-commercial positioning

- Futures markets all major contracts

- Released Friday for Tuesday data

**Commercial vs non-commercial**

- Commercials: hedgers (natural producers/users)

- Non-commercials: speculators

- Commercial long extremes: often signals bottom

- Non-commercial long extremes: often signals top

**Useful for**

- Currencies (EUR, JPY positioning)

- Commodities (oil, gold, grains)

- Treasury futures

- S&P 500 futures

### 10.5 Fund flows
**Equity fund flows**

- ICI weekly data

- Active funds (mutual funds)

- Passive (ETFs)

- Retail vs institutional differentiated

- Persistent outflows vs inflows

**ETF flows**

- State-Street Investor Confidence Index

- Strategas ETF Flows

- Sector/thematic preferences

- Retail behavior proxy

**Bond fund flows**

- TIPS flows: inflation expectations

- HY flows: risk appetite

- EM flows: global risk tolerance

**Money market flows**

- Cash on sidelines narrative

- MMF flows massive post-2022 rates rise

- Inflow pickup = risk aversion

- Outflow = deployment into risk

### 10.6 Crypto positioning
**Exchange flows**

- Inflows to exchanges: selling pressure

- Outflows to cold storage: accumulation

- Long-term holder supply changes

- On-chain data real-time

**ETF flows (post-2024)**

- Spot Bitcoin ETF inflows since January 2024

- Spot Ether ETF inflows since July 2024

- Institutional positioning proxy

- Daily data

**Futures positioning**

- CME BTC futures COT

- Deribit options open interest

- Leveraged positions

- Funding rate extremes

**Stablecoin flows**

- Mints/burns tracked

- Tether, USDC movements

- Capital flows into/out of crypto

- Leading indicator sometimes

### 10.7 Institutional positioning
**Pension fund allocations**

- Quarterly disclosures (slow)

- Target allocations published

- Rebalancing flows

- Less actionable short-term

**Hedge fund positioning**

- 13F filings (delayed)

- Prime broker data (not public)

- Fund of funds data

- Crowded trades analysis

**CTAs / trend followers**

- Allocate mechanically on trends

- Positioning data commercial (Newedge, BarclayHedge)

- Trends create procyclical positioning

- Forced reversals when trends break

**Risk parity funds**

- Target risk-weighted exposure

- Rebalancing based on volatility

- Can amplify cross-asset moves

### 10.8 Sentiment composite indicators
**CNN Fear & Greed Index**

- Composite of 7 measures

- Momentum, volatility, put/call, junk bond demand, safe haven, breadth, strength

- 0 = extreme fear, 100 = extreme greed

- Simple accessible

**Goldman Sachs Risk Appetite Index**

- Multiple asset class inputs

- Professional following

- Historically useful

**SentimentTrader composite**

- Multi-indicator composite

- Historical analysis

- Subscription service

### 10.9 Insider activity
**Corporate insider transactions**

- Form 4 SEC filings

- Insider buying: positive signal

- Insider selling: mixed signal (diversification vs pessimism)

- Aggregate trends meaningful

**Buybacks**

- Corporate share repurchases

- Pre-announced vs actual

- Blackout windows

- Historical late-cycle elevated

**IPO activity**

- Number of IPOs

- Quality of IPOs

- SPAC activity

- Retail subscription

- Elevated at cycle tops

### 10.10 F4 Positioning composite
**Structure**

Retail sentiment (25%) + options (25%) + flows (20%) + futures (15%) + crypto (10%) + insider/IPO (5%).

**Interpretation**

- F4 \> 75: extreme bullish positioning (contrarian warning)

- F4 55-75: positive positioning

- F4 30-55: mixed positioning

- F4 \< 30: extreme bearish positioning (contrarian buy signal)

**Contrarian edge**

- Extreme F4 readings are most useful

- Middle range less informative

- Historical edge stronger at extremes
